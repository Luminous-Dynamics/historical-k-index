## Part VI: Lessons from Historical Collapses for Today

### 6.1 From Bronze Age Collapse

**Lesson**: Interconnected systems create shared vulnerability.

**Modern Application**:
- Global supply chains concentrate risk
- Financial contagion spreads faster than ever
- Cyber vulnerabilities multiply with connectivity
- Need: Resilience buffers, not just efficiency

### 6.2 From Roman Decline

**Lesson**: Institutional decay is slow but irreversible without intervention.

**Modern Application**:
- Democratic backsliding often seems manageable until it isn't
- Corruption normalizes gradually
- Need: Early intervention, not waiting for crisis

### 6.3 From Maya Collapse

**Lesson**: Environmental stress amplifies coordination failures.

**Modern Application**:
- Climate change is already stressing marginal societies
- Water, food, migration pressures accumulating
- Need: Climate adaptation investment now, not later

### 6.4 From Soviet Dissolution

**Lesson**: Legitimacy crises can unfold rapidly.

**Modern Application**:
- Institutional trust can evaporate quickly
- Once lost, extremely hard to rebuild
- Need: Proactive legitimacy maintenance
