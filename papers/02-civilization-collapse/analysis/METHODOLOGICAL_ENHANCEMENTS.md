## Enhancement 2: Causal Inference Strategy

### The Problem
Correlation â‰  Causation. We observe Hâ‚ƒ declining before collapse, but:
- Did Hâ‚ƒ decline CAUSE collapse?
- Or did something else cause BOTH Hâ‚ƒ decline AND collapse?

### Solution: Multiple Causal Inference Approaches

#### Approach 1: Temporal Precedence
**Logic**: If Hâ‚ƒ decline consistently PRECEDES other harmony declines across all cases, it suggests causal priority.

**Method**:
- For each case, establish precise chronology of harmony declines
- Test: Does Hâ‚ƒ decline predict subsequent Hâ‚, Hâ‚‚, Hâ‚„ declines?
- Cross-case consistency strengthens causal claim

**Visualization**: Lag correlation analysis across cases

#### Approach 2: Mechanism Identification

**Example Mechanisms**:
- Hâ‚ƒâ†“ â†’ Elite competition â†’ Hâ‚â†“ (governance breakdown)

- Hâ‚ƒâ†“ â†’ Brain drain â†’ Hâ‚…â†“ (knowledge loss)

**Method**: Trace specific historical instances of these mechanisms in each case

#### Approach 3: Counterfactual Analysis
**Logic**: What would have happened if Hâ‚ƒ had been maintained?

**Method**:
- Identify moments when Hâ‚ƒ could have been preserved
- Analyze historical near-misses (crises that were averted)
- Compare to cases where similar Hâ‚ƒ interventions failed

**Examples**:
- Bronze Age: What if Hittite-Egyptian cooperation had extended to Mycenaeans?
- Rome: What if Diocletian's reforms had been maintained?
- Maya: What if Tikal-Calakmul rivalry had been resolved?

#### Approach 4: Control Case Comparison
**Logic**: If controls maintained Hâ‚ƒ under similar stress and survived, Hâ‚ƒ maintenance is causal.

**Method**:
- Match stressor intensity between collapse and control
- Show Hâ‚ƒ was higher in control
- Conclude Hâ‚ƒ difference explains outcome difference

#### Approach 5: Process Tracing
**Logic**: Detailed case-by-case narrative establishing causal chains.

**Method**:
- For each collapse, trace the specific sequence of events
- Identify decision points where Hâ‚ƒ failure led to cascade
- Document contemporary accounts of trust breakdown

### Causal Inference Presentation

**In manuscript**:
1. Present temporal evidence (Hâ‚ƒ first)
2. Identify mechanisms (how Hâ‚ƒ cascades)

## Enhancement 3: Reviewer Objection Pre-emption

### Anticipated Objection 1: "Scoring is Subjective"

**Pre-emption Strategy**:
1. **Explicit rubric**: Detailed criteria for each score level (already created)
2. **Evidence documentation**: Every score linked to specific evidence
3. **Sensitivity analysis**: Show results hold across scoring variations
4. **Inter-coder reliability**: If possible, have domain expert score independently
5. **Transparency**: All scores and evidence in supplementary materials

**Manuscript Language**:
> "We acknowledge the semi-quantitative nature of historical harmony reconstruction. To address this, we developed detailed scoring rubrics (Supplementary Materials S1), documented all evidence underlying each score (S2), and conducted extensive sensitivity analyses showing our conclusions are robust to reasonable scoring variations (S3)."

### Anticipated Objection 2: "Case Selection is Cherry-Picked"

**Pre-emption Strategy**:
1. **Explicit criteria**: State case selection criteria upfront
2. **Varied cases**: Different time periods, geographies, society types
3. **Control cases**: Not just collapsesâ€”also survivals
4. **Acknowledge limitations**: Note which cases we couldn't include and why

**Selection Criteria (explicit)**:
- Clear collapse trajectory (not gradual transformation)
- Sufficient evidence for harmony reconstruction
- Scholarly consensus on collapse occurrence
- Geographic/temporal diversity

**Manuscript Language**:
> "We selected cases based on four criteria: clear collapse trajectory, sufficient historical evidence, scholarly consensus, and diversity across time and geography. We acknowledge that other cases (e.g., Akkadian Empire, Khmer Empire, Han Dynasty) merit analysis and represent important directions for future research."

### Anticipated Objection 3: "Modern Applicability is Speculative"

**Pre-emption Strategy**:
1. **Modest claims**: Frame modern implications as hypotheses, not conclusions
2. **Soviet case bridge**: Use quantitative modern case to validate methodology
3. **Explicit limitations**: Acknowledge differences between historical and modern contexts
4. **Future research**: Position Paper 3 (Modern Fragility) as proper modern analysis

**Manuscript Language**:
> "While we propose that coordination dynamics observed in historical collapses may inform modern fragility assessment, we emphasize that contemporary societies differ significantly in complexity, interconnection, and response capacity. The Soviet case, with its quantitative data, provides a methodological bridge, but systematic modern analysis requires dedicated study (see our companion paper on Modern Fragility Assessment)."

### Anticipated Objection 4: "Why These Seven Harmonies?"

**Pre-emption Strategy**:
1. **Reference Paper 1**: Theoretical justification already established
2. **Alternative formulations**: Test if results hold with different harmony definitions
3. **Principal component analysis**: Show harmonies capture distinct variance

**Manuscript Language**:
> "The seven-harmony framework is theoretically grounded in our companion paper (Stoltz 2025) and empirically validated against modern data. Sensitivity analyses using alternative harmony definitions yield consistent results (Supplementary Materials S4)."

### Anticipated Objection 5: "Sample Size Too Small"

**Pre-emption Strategy**:
1. **Depth over breadth**: Justify intensive case study approach
2. **Pattern consistency**: Show patterns hold across diverse cases
3. **Acknowledge limitation**: More cases would strengthen findings
4. **Future research**: Invite community to apply framework to additional cases

**Manuscript Language**:
> "We prioritize depth of analysis over breadth of cases, enabling detailed harmony reconstruction and process tracing. The consistency of patterns across four diverse casesâ€”spanning 3,000 years and three continentsâ€”suggests generalizability, though additional cases would strengthen our conclusions."
