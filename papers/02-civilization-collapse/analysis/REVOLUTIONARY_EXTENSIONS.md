## Extension 3: Early Warning Signals (Complexity Science Application)

### Critical Transitions Theory

Drawing from Scheffer et al. (2012), complex systems show **early warning signals** before tipping points:

1. **Critical Slowing Down** - Recovery from perturbations takes longer
2. **Increased Variance** - Fluctuations become larger
3. **Increased Autocorrelation** - State becomes "sticky"
4. **Flickering** - System alternates between states

### Application to K-Index

**Hypothesis**: Before collapse, K(t) shows early warning signals

**Testable Predictions**:
| Signal | Measurement | Expected Pattern |
|--------|-------------|------------------|
| Slowing down | Recovery time from crises | Increases before collapse |
| Variance | Standard deviation of Hâ‚ƒ | Increases before collapse |
| Autocorrelation | Lag-1 correlation | Increases toward 1 |
| Flickering | Alternating high/low Hâ‚ƒ | Appears before collapse |

### Data Requirements

For each case:
- High-resolution Hâ‚ƒ estimates (annual or better for Soviet case)
- Multiple perturbation events to measure recovery
- Variance and autocorrelation calculations

### Why This Is Revolutionary

1. **Predictive Framework** - Not just post-hoc explanation
2. **Generic Indicators** - Same signals across diverse collapses
3. **Modern Application** - Can apply to current societies
4. **Intervention Trigger** - When signals appear, act immediately
