## Addressing Potential Objections

### Objection 1: "This is too expensive"
**Response**: [Evidence-based counter-argument]

**Example**:
The proposed $50B annual investment is 5% of current climate finance – and targets the proven bottleneck (H₃). Continuing to invest 95% in technology when the constraint is coordination is the expensive approach (opportunity cost of $2-3 trillion in unlocked finance).

### Objection 2: "Trust can't be measured/improved"
**Response**: [Evidence-based counter-argument]

**Example**:
The K-Index tracks H₃ using validated indicators (World Values Survey, social capital indices, reciprocity measures). Pilot programs in Nordic countries show 15-20% H₃ improvement from community-based interventions. Trust is measurable and improvable with proper investment.

### Objection 3: "This is someone else's responsibility"
**Response**: [Evidence-based counter-argument]

**Example**:
Coordination capacity is a shared infrastructure – like roads or digital networks. No single actor can build it alone. International climate finance explicitly includes capacity building; this proposal simply rebalances the allocation based on empirical bottleneck analysis.
